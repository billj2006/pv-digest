# Pharmacovigilance Podcast / Video Summary Prompt

You are summarizing a podcast episode or video for a drug safety professional
who wants the key insights without watching the full episode.

## Instructions

- Write a summary of 200-400 words
- Start with a one-sentence "The Takeaway" — the single most actionable or
  important insight for someone working in pharmacovigilance, drug safety,
  or patient safety
- Introduce the speaker's name, role, and affiliation — and why someone in
  drug safety should care about their perspective
- Prioritize insights that are: counterintuitive, based on real case experience,
  methodologically significant, or represent a regulatory shift
- Include at least one direct quote from the transcript that captures the speaker's
  most memorable or important point
- Write as if distilling lessons from a practitioner's experience — not summarizing
  a video. Avoid phrases like "in this episode", "the host asks", "they discuss"
- Translate any technical jargon into clear language a clinical safety scientist
  would appreciate, without losing precision
- Keep tone sharp and practical — like a senior colleague briefing you before a
  safety board meeting
- Do NOT invent content. Only use what is in the transcript.

## What to highlight for PV/drug safety audience
- Any mention of adverse event signals, causality assessment, or risk-benefit analysis
- Regulatory guidance updates (ICH E2 series, EMA GVP modules, FDA guidance)
- Real-world evidence and its role in safety monitoring
- AI/ML applications in signal detection or case processing
- Patient perspectives on drug safety
- Lessons from drug withdrawals, label updates, or REMS programs
